## The principles behind every good cold email

1. **Subject line in 4 words or fewer.** Long subjects get filtered or skipped.
2. **First line cannot be "I hope this finds you well."** That sentence is a death warrant.
3. **One ask per email.** If you ask for an intro AND a meeting AND feedback, you'll get nothing.
4. **Show research in the first 2 lines.** Prove you actually know who they are.
5. **Make the next step trivially small.** "15 min next Tuesday at 10?" beats "do you have time to chat?"
6. **Send between Tuesday–Thursday, 7–10am their time.** Inboxes are most receptive then.
7. **Follow up 3 times.** 80% of replies come from follow-ups.

## B2B Sales Cold Email

### The "specific observation" template

```
Subject: quick idea for [company]

Hey [first name],

Saw [specific signal — they hired a head of growth, launched a feature, posted on LinkedIn about X]. Congrats.

We help [similar company type] with [specific outcome — not "we help with X"]. Most of them save [concrete number] within [timeframe].

Worth a 15-min look next Tuesday at 10am PT?

[your name]

[1-line credibility — "ex-stripe, building [tool] used by [N] [persona]"]
```

### The "tiny ask" template

```
Subject: 1 question

Hey [first name],

Quick one: how are you handling [specific pain at their stage]?

Asking because we just built something for [similar company type] and want to make sure it solves the right problem.

[your name]
```

### Why these work

Specific signal in line 1 proves you're not blasting. The ask is tiny and tied to their world, not yours. The CTA is a calendar suggestion, not "let me know if interested."

## Investor Outreach

### Cold reach to a partner you've never met

```
Subject: [company] — [traction headline]

Hi [first name],

[Company] is [one-sentence what we do]. We just [biggest concrete proof point — $X MRR, growth rate, customer name, or product win].

Three reasons I'm reaching out to you specifically:

1. You led [recent investment in similar space]
2. You wrote [specific essay / tweet] about [topic relevant to us]
3. [Mutual connection / portfolio company we know]

Deck attached. 30 min next week if it resonates?

[your name]
```

### Warm intro request to a portco founder

```
Subject: intro to [investor first name]?

Hey [founder],

Long time no chat — saw the [recent thing they did]. Awesome.

Quick ask: would you be open to introducing me to [investor name] at [firm]? We're [one-line] and just hit [concrete proof point]. They've been investing in [space] and I think the timing makes sense.

Happy to send a forwardable so you can decide if it's a fit. Total of 60 seconds of your time.

Thanks either way.

[your name]
```

### Forwardable for the warm intro

```
Subject: [Company] - [stage] - [traction in one phrase]

Hi [investor first name] — [founder] suggested we connect.

[Company] helps [persona] [outcome]. Three numbers:
- [Metric 1 with magnitude]
- [Metric 2 with magnitude]
- [Metric 3 with magnitude]

We're raising a [amount] [round type] led by [or solo, depending] to [specific milestone we're funding to].

Deck: [link]
Demo (90 sec): [link]

If interesting, I'd love 30 min. If not, no worries — would love your honest take regardless.

[your name]
```

## Investor Update Emails

Send a monthly update from day 1, even if nobody asked. Future investors will read every one before they meet you.

### Monthly update structure

```
Subject: [Company] — [Month Year] update

TL;DR
- [3 one-line bullets — biggest wins, biggest challenges]

Key metrics (vs last month)
- ARR / MRR: $X (+Y% MoM)
- Active users: [N] (+/-Z%)
- [North star metric]: X

Wins
- [Specific customer / product / hire]
- [Specific customer / product / hire]
- [Specific customer / product / hire]

Challenges & asks
- [Specific challenge]. Asks: intros to [type of person] / hiring [role] / advice on [topic]

What's next
- [Headline goal for next month]

Thanks for the support,
[your name]
```

## Press / Journalist Pitch

### The "story angle" pitch

```
Subject: [angle, 6 words max — "AI dictation 5x faster than Whisper"]

Hi [first name],

Saw your piece on [recent article they wrote — must be specific]. Loved [specific point they made].

I'm building [company]. We just [news hook — launch / round / milestone] and I think there's a story angle that fits your beat:

[1-2 sentences framing the story — not your product. The story is the angle, your product is just the example]

Some specifics:
- [Number that supports the angle]
- [Customer / quote]
- [Why now]

Happy to send a brief, set up a call, or just answer a question or two. No expectation either way.

[your name]
```

## Partnership Outreach

### To a complementary tool's founder

```
Subject: idea for [their company] x [your company]

Hey [first name],

We're both building for [shared persona]. [Their tool] does [X]. [Your tool] does [Y]. Our customers keep asking us for [the integration / co-marketing thing that would help both of us].

Three concrete ideas:
1. [Specific integration]
2. [Co-marketed webinar / content / list]
3. [Affiliate / referral mechanic]

Worth 20 min next week to see if any are interesting?

[your name]
```

## Hiring / Recruiting Reach-out

### To a passive senior candidate

```
Subject: 30 sec — [your company]?

Hi [first name],

I run [company]. We're [one line — funding, stage, team size]. [One-line product].

I'm hiring [role]. Two reasons I'm reaching out specifically:
1. [Specific thing about their work — a project, post, or repo]
2. [Specific reason this role would interest them — autonomy, equity, mission]

If you're even slightly curious, I'd love 20 min — no pressure to interview. Just want to share what we're building and learn what you're optimizing for.

[your name]
```

## Customer Interview Request

### Asking your existing user for 20 minutes

```
Subject: 20 min for $50?

Hi [first name],

You signed up for [product] [N days/weeks] ago. I'm the founder, and I'm trying to make this thing dramatically better.

Would you be up for a 20-min call where I just ask you questions about your workflow? I'll send you a $50 gift card afterwards as thanks.

Tuesday 10am or Wednesday 2pm work?

[your name]
```

### Asking a non-user (problem interview)

```
Subject: research interview ($75) for [persona]

Hi [first name],

Quick context: I'm researching how [persona] handle [specific workflow]. I'm not selling anything — pure problem research.

Looking for 20 minutes on a call. I'll send a $75 gift card after, no strings.

Three questions to give you a sense:
- Walk me through how you currently [action]
- What part of that frustrates you most?
- Last time you tried to fix it, what happened?

Tuesday or Wednesday?

[your name]
```

## Re-engagement / Trial Conversion

### Trial expired, no conversion

```
Subject: [first name] — what stopped you?

Hi [first name],

You started a trial of [product] [N] days ago and didn't convert. Totally fine — but I'm trying to understand why so we can build a better version.

One question: was it the product, the price, the timing, or something else?

Reply with a single word and I'll leave it there. (Or no reply, no problem.)

[your name]
```

## Backlink / SEO Outreach

### The skyscraper approach

```
Subject: thought you'd find this useful

Hi [first name],

I read your post on [topic] — specifically liked [specific detail].

I just published [your resource] which goes deeper on [adjacent angle they didn't cover]. It might be a good addition to your piece if you're ever updating it.

[Link]

Either way, thanks for the original — it influenced what I wrote.

[your name]
```

## Speaker / Podcast Outreach

### Pitching yourself as a guest

```
Subject: guest idea — [topic that fits their show]

Hi [first name],

Long-time listener. Loved the [specific recent episode] — especially [specific moment].

I'd love to come on and talk about [topic that fits their audience]. Three angles I could cover:

1. [Angle 1 — concrete, with one specific story]
2. [Angle 2 — concrete, with one specific story]
3. [Angle 3 — concrete, with one specific story]

I've been on [credibility — other shows, where to verify I'm not a bad guest].

If any of those resonate, happy to send more detail. If not, no worries — keep up the great work.

[your name]
```

## Follow-up cadence that converts

For every cold email above:

- **Day 0:** Send original
- **Day 3:** Reply to your own thread with a one-liner: "Bumping this in case it got buried."
- **Day 7:** Reply with a slightly different angle — new data point, customer quote, or relevant news.
- **Day 14:** Final email — "Last note from me. If now isn't right, would you be open to chatting in 6 months?"

After that, walk away for a quarter. People's needs change.

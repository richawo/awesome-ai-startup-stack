## Two weeks before launch

### Build a list of people who'll show up

Launches don't go viral on their own. They get pushed over the edge by ~50 people who knew the launch was coming. Two weeks out, build that list:

- 30 friends, founders, and ex-colleagues — DM each personally
- 10 people in your target customer segment who already follow you
- 5 friendly creators / newsletters / journalists who might amplify
- Any existing waitlist or beta users

DM each one *individually*. Tell them what's launching and the day. Ask if they'd help. Do not BCC.

### Pick your day

- **Best day for Product Hunt:** Tuesday or Wednesday. Avoid Mondays (heavy launch day, high competition) and Sundays (low traffic).
- **Best day for Hacker News:** Tuesday morning ~9am EST. Weekends are dead.
- **Best day for Twitter/X:** Tuesday or Wednesday morning.
- **Avoid:** US holidays, the week of major Apple/Google/OpenAI keynotes, election weeks.

### Polish the basics

- Working homepage with a clear one-liner above the fold
- Demo video, 60 seconds, no music, just narration
- Pricing page (even if it's just "free for now")
- One-paragraph "About" + founder photo
- Social proof — at least one customer quote or screenshot
- Twitter / LinkedIn presence cleaned up

## One week before launch

### Build assets in advance

- 1 launch tweet (your tweet thread, drafted)
- 3 customer-quote tweets (text + screenshot)
- 1 launch blog post (1000–2000 words, the "why we built this" story)
- 1 newsletter to your existing list
- 1 personal email draft to friends/supporters
- 5 different social-card images for the launch (1 per channel)

### Coordinate with your supporters

Send a single short email to your 50-person supporter list:

> Hey — [tool] launches on [day] at [time]. I'd love your support.
>
> Three ways that help most (in order):
> 1. Upvote on Product Hunt: [link]
> 2. Retweet this: [link to your launch tweet]
> 3. Share with one person who'd benefit
>
> No pressure if today's not the day.

Don't ask for "comments" or "shares" — that triggers Reddit/PH spam filters.

## The day before launch

### Schedule everything

- Schedule your launch tweet for 7am PT (or your local primetime)
- Schedule the Product Hunt post for 12:01am PT (this is the cutoff)
- Pre-write 5 follow-up tweets
- Pre-write your "answers to common questions" doc
- Make sure analytics, signup flow, and payments are working

### Send last reminder

A short text, not an email:

> "[tool] launches in 12 hours. If you're around tomorrow morning, link will be at [your twitter handle]. Thanks 🙏"

## Launch day — hour by hour

### 12:01am PT — Submit Product Hunt

- Submit the post (yourself, with your account — never use a hunter)
- Use the same product description as your homepage
- First image: your hero illustration / clean product shot
- Subsequent images: feature screenshots
- Tagline: 8 words max, no buzzwords, just what it does
- Have your team / first 5 supporters ready to upvote in the first hour

### 6am PT — Submit Hacker News (Show HN)

- Title format: `Show HN: [Tool] – [one-sentence description]`
- Submit yourself, with your real account (not a sock puppet)
- Post the link to your homepage, not the blog post
- Comment yourself within 5 min: "Hi HN, founder here. [Origin story / context / key technical decision]. Happy to answer questions."
- Hacker News penalizes obvious self-promotion. The comment matters as much as the post.

### 7am PT — Twitter / X launch thread

Structure that works:

```
1/ Today we're launching [tool] — [one-line description].

[Image or 30s video]

[Link]

Why we built it: 🧵

2/ The problem we kept hitting…

3/ Why existing tools didn't solve it…

4/ What we built…

5/ How it works (technical)…

6/ Who it's for…

7/ The two things I'm proudest of…

8/ What's next…

9/ Try it free at [link]. Replies / questions / roasts welcome.
```

### 8am PT — Personal outreach

- Send your prepared email to your 50-person list
- Slack/iMessage 5 individual close friends
- Post in any private founder Slacks you're in (with the host's permission)

### 9am PT — Reddit posts

- Pick 1–2 subreddits maximum (start with [r/SideProject](https://www.reddit.com/r/SideProject/))
- Use the "I built X because of Y" format from the [subreddits guide](startup-subreddits.md)
- Reply to every comment within 10 minutes for the first 3 hours

### 10am PT — LinkedIn + IndieHackers

- LinkedIn post — long-form, story-led, drop the link in the first comment (algorithm penalizes external links in posts)
- IndieHackers — milestone post + product page

### Throughout the day

- **Reply to every comment everywhere within 15 minutes.** This is the single highest-leverage thing you can do. Founders who show up in comments win launches.
- **Don't ask for upvotes anywhere.** Both PH and HN punish this hard.
- **Have your support email open.** New users will trigger bugs; fix them in real time.
- **Tweet new milestones.** "Just hit #3 on Product Hunt 🙏" — short, frequent, grateful tweets keep the wave alive.
- **Keep an eye on your servers.** Real launches break things.

### End of day

- Tweet a thank-you with the day's results
- Email your supporter list with the outcome (whether you finished #1 or #45)
- Write down everything that went wrong — for next time

## Product Hunt specifics

- **Tagline (under 60 chars):** What it does, no buzzwords. "Voice typing for Mac, faster than Wispr." Beats "Reimagining productivity with AI."
- **Description (under 260 chars):** Plain-English version of the tagline + main differentiator + free/paid status.
- **First comment (yours):** Origin story, what's free vs paid, how to give feedback. Do this within 5 min of going live.
- **Maker comment:** Be present in the thread for the first 24 hours. Reply to everything.
- **Answer the "What makes it different from X?" question proactively** in your first comment.

## Hacker News specifics

- **Title:** `Show HN: [Tool] – [thing]`. Don't use marketing language.
- **First comment:** Yours, within 5 min, explaining context. Mention what you built it on if technical.
- **Be honest.** HN can smell a press release from orbit. If your product has limits, say so up front.
- **Don't beg for upvotes.** A single complaint to mods will sink your post.
- **Engage technical questions deeply.** People who comment on Show HN are often the best users.

## Reddit specifics

- See full Reddit playbook in [startup-subreddits.md](startup-subreddits.md).
- Pick 1–2 subreddits per day, max. Don't crosspost.
- Always read the subreddit's rules before posting.

## The week after

- **Day 2:** Post results / reflection thread on Twitter and IndieHackers.
- **Day 3:** Email everyone who signed up but didn't activate. Personal note from the founder.
- **Day 7:** Write a "lessons from the launch" blog post / newsletter.
- **Week 2:** Pitch the post-launch story to journalists. Real numbers + lessons sells better than the launch itself.
- **Week 4:** First paid acquisition test (Twitter Ads, Reddit Ads, Google Search). Use the launch traffic to estimate CAC.

## What "successful" looks like by stage

| Channel        | "OK"          | "Great"          | "Best in class"   |
| -------------- | ------------- | ---------------- | ----------------- |
| Product Hunt   | Top 10        | Top 3            | #1 of the day     |
| Hacker News    | Front page    | Top 10 of day    | Top 3 of week     |
| Reddit (sub)   | 50 upvotes    | 200 upvotes      | Pinned by mods    |
| Twitter        | 5k impressions| 50k impressions  | 500k impressions  |
| Signups (24h)  | 100           | 1,000            | 10,000            |

If you hit "OK" on most channels, your launch was a success. Compounding follow-up beats one viral spike.

## Books — Startups and entrepreneurship

- **Zero to One** — Peter Thiel. On building monopolies and contrarian thinking.
- **The Hard Thing About Hard Things** — Ben Horowitz. On running a company through difficult decisions.
- **High Output Management** — Andy Grove. On management as leverage.
- **The Lean Startup** — Eric Ries. On validated learning and build-measure-learn.
- **The Mom Test** — Rob Fitzpatrick. On customer interviews.
- **Good Strategy / Bad Strategy** — Richard Rumelt. On strategy.
- **Crossing the Chasm** — Geoffrey Moore. On the tech adoption lifecycle.
- **The Innovator's Dilemma** — Clayton Christensen. On disruption.
- **The Innovator's Solution** — Clayton Christensen. On responding to disruption.
- **Founders at Work** — Jessica Livingston. Interviews with startup founders.
- **Hackers and Painters** — Paul Graham. Essays on startups and software.
- **Venture Deals** — Brad Feld and Jason Mendelson. On VC fundraising terms.
- **Secrets of Sand Hill Road** — Scott Kupor. On VC and fundraising.

## Books — Building products

- **Inspired** — Marty Cagan. On product management.
- **Continuous Discovery Habits** — Teresa Torres. On interview-driven product processes.
- **Hooked** — Nir Eyal. On habit-forming product design.
- **The Design of Everyday Things** — Don Norman. On usability and affordances.
- **Don't Make Me Think** — Steve Krug. On web usability.
- **Refactoring UI** — Adam Wathan & Steve Schoger. On visual design.
- **Shape Up** — Ryan Singer / Basecamp. On product cycles and shaping work.
- **The Build Trap** — Melissa Perri. On outcome-driven product orgs.

## Books — Distribution and growth

- **Traction** — Gabriel Weinberg & Justin Mares. On the 19 acquisition channels.
- **Obviously Awesome** — April Dunford. On positioning.
- **Made to Stick** — Chip & Dan Heath. On ideas that spread.
- **Influence** — Robert Cialdini. On persuasion.
- **Pre-Suasion** — Robert Cialdini. On the framing that precedes persuasion.
- **The Tipping Point** — Malcolm Gladwell. On how ideas spread.
- **Purple Cow** — Seth Godin. On differentiation.
- **Marketing Made Simple** — Donald Miller. On the StoryBrand framework.
- **The 22 Immutable Laws of Marketing** — Al Ries & Jack Trout. On marketing principles.

## Books — Leadership and people

- **Radical Candor** — Kim Scott. On feedback and management.
- **Multipliers** — Liz Wiseman. On leaders who amplify their teams.
- **The Five Dysfunctions of a Team** — Patrick Lencioni. On team dynamics.
- **An Elegant Puzzle** — Will Larson. On engineering management at scale.
- **The Manager's Path** — Camille Fournier. On engineering leadership progression.
- **Crucial Conversations** — Patterson et al. On having difficult conversations.
- **Never Split the Difference** — Chris Voss. On negotiation.
- **No Rules Rules** — Reed Hastings & Erin Meyer. On Netflix's culture.
- **Powerful** — Patty McCord. On Netflix's HR practices.

## Books — Decision-making and thinking

- **Thinking, Fast and Slow** — Daniel Kahneman. On cognitive biases and System 1/System 2.
- **Superforecasting** — Philip Tetlock. On forecasting.
- **The Black Swan** — Nassim Taleb. On tail risk.
- **Antifragile** — Nassim Taleb. On systems that gain from disorder.
- **The Beginning of Infinity** — David Deutsch. On knowledge creation.
- **Seeking Wisdom** — Peter Bevelin. On Charlie Munger's mental models.
- **Poor Charlie's Almanack** — Charlie Munger. On decision-making.

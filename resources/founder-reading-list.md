## Books — The canon

These keep showing up on every founder's bookshelf. There's a reason.

- **Zero to One** — Peter Thiel. Contrarian thinking, monopolies, "what important truth do very few people agree with you on?"
- **The Hard Thing About Hard Things** — Ben Horowitz. The CEO job nobody warns you about. Required reading the day you have to fire someone.
- **High Output Management** — Andy Grove. Still the best management book ever written. Operations as leverage.
- **The Lean Startup** — Eric Ries. Validated learning, build-measure-learn. Read it once, reference it forever.
- **The Mom Test** — Rob Fitzpatrick. How to actually do customer interviews. ~100 pages. Read it before your next 10 user calls.
- **Good Strategy / Bad Strategy** — Richard Rumelt. What strategy actually is, vs. what people call strategy.
- **Crossing the Chasm** — Geoffrey Moore. Tech adoption lifecycle. Why "early adopter excitement" doesn't translate to mainstream.
- **The Innovator's Dilemma** — Clayton Christensen. Why incumbents lose. Useful even if you're not disrupting Kodak.
- **The Innovator's Solution** — Christensen's follow-up. Even better, less famous.
- **Founders at Work** — Jessica Livingston. Interviews with founders of the dot-com era. Pattern-matches your messy first year.
- **Hackers and Painters** — Paul Graham. Startups as craft.

## Books — On building products

- **Inspired** — Marty Cagan. How modern product teams actually work.
- **Continuous Discovery Habits** — Teresa Torres. The interview-driven product process.
- **Hooked** — Nir Eyal. Building habit-forming products.
- **The Design of Everyday Things** — Don Norman. The book that taught a generation of PMs and designers what affordances are.
- **Don't Make Me Think** — Steve Krug. Web usability bible, still relevant.
- **Refactoring UI** — Adam Wathan & Steve Schoger. Practical visual-design principles for non-designers. Great for engineers shipping UI.
- **Shape Up** — Ryan Singer / Basecamp. The 6-week-cycle product methodology.
- **The Build Trap** — Melissa Perri. Why most product orgs ship features no one uses.

## Books — On distribution and growth

- **Traction** — Gabriel Weinberg & Justin Mares. The 19 channels every startup should test, and how to pick.
- **Obviously Awesome** — April Dunford. Positioning, the missing piece between product and marketing.
- **Made to Stick** — Chip & Dan Heath. Why some ideas spread and others die. Best book on storytelling for founders.
- **Influence** — Robert Cialdini. The six levers of persuasion. Read it for marketing, re-read it for sales.
- **Pre-Suasion** — Cialdini's follow-up. About what you do *before* you ask.
- **The Tipping Point** — Malcolm Gladwell. Connectors, mavens, salesmen. Useful even if you're skeptical of Gladwell.
- **Purple Cow** — Seth Godin. Be remarkable or be ignored.
- **Marketing Made Simple** — Donald Miller. The StoryBrand framework. Useful for clarifying your pitch.
- **The 22 Immutable Laws of Marketing** — Al Ries & Jack Trout. Old, still right.

## Books — On leadership and people

- **Radical Candor** — Kim Scott. Care personally, challenge directly. The framework every first-time manager needs.
- **Multipliers** — Liz Wiseman. Leaders who make their teams smarter vs. dumber.
- **The Five Dysfunctions of a Team** — Patrick Lencioni. Trust → Conflict → Commitment → Accountability → Results.
- **An Elegant Puzzle** — Will Larson. Engineering management at scale, written by someone who's done it.
- **The Manager's Path** — Camille Fournier. From senior IC to CTO.
- **Crucial Conversations** — Patterson et al. How to have hard conversations without ruining the relationship.
- **Never Split the Difference** — Chris Voss. Negotiation by an FBI hostage negotiator. Useful in every customer call you'll ever do.
- **No Rules Rules** — Reed Hastings & Erin Meyer. Inside Netflix's "talent density" culture.
- **Powerful** — Patty McCord. The Netflix HR book. Compensation, hiring, firing, the unsentimental version.

## Books — On thinking clearly

- **Thinking, Fast and Slow** — Daniel Kahneman. System 1 vs. System 2. The book on cognitive biases.
- **Superforecasting** — Philip Tetlock. The traits of people who predict the future better than experts.
- **The Black Swan** — Nassim Taleb. Why we under-rate tail risk.
- **Antifragile** — Taleb's better book. Things that gain from disorder.
- **The Beginning of Infinity** — David Deutsch. Optimism as a stance, knowledge creation as the engine.
- **Seeking Wisdom** — Peter Bevelin. A summary-of-summaries on Charlie Munger's mental models.
- **Poor Charlie's Almanack** — Charlie Munger's collected wisdom. Decision-making as a discipline.

## Courses for AI builders

Hands-on courses worth the time. Skip the rest — most "AI courses" are recycled blog posts.

- **[28 Days of Lovable](https://february.lovable.app/)** — Free 28-day video course on building full-stack apps with Lovable's AI builder. Covers prompting, design, and shipping real apps. The single best on-ramp to AI app building if you've never used Lovable, Bolt, or v0.
- **[DeepLearning.AI Short Courses](https://www.deeplearning.ai/short-courses/)** — Andrew Ng's free short courses on prompt engineering, RAG, agents, evals, and more. Each one is 1–2 hours, all worth doing.
- **[Latent Space University](https://www.latent.space/)** — swyx's AI engineering curriculum (free + paid).
- **[Anthropic's Prompt Engineering Tutorial](https://github.com/anthropics/courses)** — Free, hands-on, tightly written. The fastest way to actually understand prompts.
- **[Hugging Face Courses](https://huggingface.co/learn)** — Free courses on NLP, deep learning, audio, and reinforcement learning.
- **[Fast.ai](https://www.fast.ai/)** — Jeremy Howard's free deep learning course. Code-first, world-class.
- **[Build Your Own X (GitHub)](https://github.com/codecrafters-io/build-your-own-x)** — Not a course but a master list of "build your own DB / OS / language" tutorials. Foundational.
- **[Y Combinator Startup School](https://www.startupschool.org/)** — Free course on starting a startup. Still the most concentrated YC content available.
- **[Stanford CS25](https://web.stanford.edu/class/cs25/)** — Free Stanford course on transformers (videos on YouTube).

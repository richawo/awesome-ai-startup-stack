## How to read this list

Submitting to directories does two things:

1. **Drives referral traffic** — only Tier 1 directories really do this anymore.
2. **Backlinks for SEO** — most directories give you a backlink, which slowly compounds your domain authority.

Be honest about which one you're optimizing for. A scattershot submission to 100 directories takes 8+ hours and might give you 30 visitors — but it does build backlinks. A focused launch on the 5 best ones takes a day and can drive thousands of users in 24 hours.

This list is tiered. Start at the top.

## Tier 1 — The launches that matter

These are the ones that drive real traffic, real users, and real backlinks. If you only have a day to spend launching, spend it here.

- **[Product Hunt](https://www.producthunt.com)** — The biggest single-day spike for any consumer-facing tool. A top-3 finish gets you ~5–20k visitors and significant backlinks. Plan it weeks in advance — see [launch-day-playbook.md](launch-day-playbook.md).
- **[Hacker News (Show HN)](https://news.ycombinator.com/show)** — Hard to crack, but a front-page HN brings developer-heavy traffic that converts well for technical AI tools. Be present in the comments or it dies.
- **[Indie Hackers Products](https://www.indiehackers.com/products)** — Posts to the milestones feed get great engagement from other builders. Best for transparent revenue / lessons posts.
- **[BetaList](https://betalist.com)** — Early-stage product directory. Submit before launch to build a waitlist; submit after to claim the listing. ~$129 for fast-track.
- **[There's An AI For That](https://theresanaiforthat.com)** — The largest AI tool directory by traffic (5M+ monthly visits). Free submission, paid for fast-track. The single biggest AI-specific traffic source.
- **[Future Tools](https://www.futuretools.io)** — Matt Wolfe's curated AI directory. High-quality audience. Submit free; gets featured if good.
- **[Futurepedia](https://www.futurepedia.io)** — Big AI tool aggregator with strong SEO. Free + paid listings.
- **[G2](https://www.g2.com)** *(if you're B2B SaaS)* — The largest B2B software review site. Listings pay back over years through SEO and review-driven sales.
- **[Capterra / GetApp / Software Advice](https://www.capterra.com)** *(if you're B2B SaaS)* — Gartner-owned trio. Same playbook as G2.
- **[StackShare](https://stackshare.io)** *(if you're a dev tool)* — Submit your tech stack + your tool. Generates authority backlinks and dev visibility.

## Submit-once, syndicate-everywhere tools

These tools take one submission and post you to dozens of directories. Time-savers if you accept the trade of less control.

- **[TinyLaunch](https://www.tinylaunch.com)** — Submit once; syndicates across many directories. The best of this category right now.
- **[Listing Bott](https://listingbott.com)** — Auto-submits to ~100+ directories.
- **[Promote Project](https://promoteproject.com)** — Submit to a list of startup directories.
- **[Get Listed AI](https://getlistedai.com)** — AI startup syndicator.
- **[Submit My Startup](https://submitmystartup.com)** — Multi-directory submission service.

A practical tip: most "submit-everywhere" tools end up listing you on directories that get ~0 organic traffic. They're 80% backlinks, 20% real visibility. Set expectations accordingly.

## How to actually submit (playbook)

When you submit to 50+ directories, you'll fill the same fields 50+ times. Build a single source-of-truth doc up front:

### Your "launch one-pager" should contain

- **Product name** and **tagline** (under 60 characters, no buzzwords)
- **One-line description** (160 chars — Twitter-bio-length)
- **Long description** (~500 words)
- **Logo** (PNG, transparent, 256×256 + 1024×1024 versions)
- **Screenshots** (4–8, hero shot first, with annotations if helpful)
- **Demo video** (60 sec, no music, just narration)
- **Pricing** (clear; "free with paid plan" or "starts at $X/mo")
- **Categories / tags** (pick 3–5 that fit your product)
- **Founder bio** (1 sentence + Twitter handle)
- **Contact email**

Keep this in a Notion or Google Doc. Copy-paste into every form.

### Submission rhythm

- **Day 0 (launch day):** Tier 1 only — Product Hunt, HN, Indie Hackers, your top 2 niche directories.
- **Day 2–7:** Top 10 AI / SaaS directories (TAAFT, Future Tools, G2 if applicable, etc.).
- **Day 8–30:** Bulk submit to Tier 2/3 via [TinyLaunch](https://www.tinylaunch.com) or [Listing Bott](https://listingbott.com).
- **Ongoing:** As you ship features and hit milestones, re-launch on Product Hunt with v2/v3, and re-pitch newsletters with new angles.

### Pitch newsletters with a story, not a press release

Newsletter editors get 50 launch pitches a day. The ones they cover have a *story angle* — usually something contrarian, surprising, or technical. Pitch the angle, not the launch.

Bad: "We launched [tool], it does X." 
Good: "We rebuilt [common workflow] using only on-device models — here's why nobody's tried this before, and here's the latency comparison."

## What's worth skipping

A short, brutal list:

- **Pay-to-list directories with no visible audience.** If a directory is asking for $99 to list and you can't find any of their featured tools on Google, walk away.
- **AI tool directories with 50k+ "tools" and no editorial filter.** They list everything, so being on them means nothing.
- **Backlink-only directories.** A directory with no real visitors gives you a dofollow link, which Google partly devalues anyway. They're not worth more than 5 minutes of your time each.
- **Awards that ask for fees to "consider" you.** Unless it's Awwwards or similar prestige, skip.
- **Directories that lock your listing behind their app/extension install.** Nobody installs.
- **Press release distribution services for "$199 syndicated to 200 outlets."** The 200 outlets are robots.

If you're spending more than $100 to be listed somewhere, the directory should have either obvious recurring traffic, or genuine editorial selection.

## A realistic launch timeline

A realistic launch using this list looks like:

| When            | Action                                                                             |
| --------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 2 weeks before  | Submit to BetaList (build waitlist), schedule Product Hunt launch                  |
| 1 week before   | Pre-write submissions in your launch one-pager doc                                 |
| Day 0           | Product Hunt + Show HN + IndieHackers + top niche AI directory + Twitter           |
| Day 1–3         | TAAFT, Futurepedia, Future Tools, the rest of Tier 1                               |
| Day 4–7         | AI Top Tools, Toolify, Insidr, Top AI Tools, AI Scout                              |
| Week 2          | G2 / Capterra / GetApp (if B2B), StackShare (if devtool), AlternativeTo            |
| Week 2          | TinyLaunch / Listing Bott bulk submission for backlinks                            |
| Week 3          | Newsletter outreach with story angles (not launch press)                           |
| Ongoing         | Update listings as you ship; re-launch on PH for major v2 milestones               |

Total time investment: 8–15 hours of focused work. Total expected traffic: 5k–50k visitors over the first month, depending on product fit and which Tier 1 launches land.

That's it. Now stop reading and go submit.
